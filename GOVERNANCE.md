# Governance

## Overview

Zuri Market is a proprietary platform owned and led by Chris Odhiambo.
This document describes how decisions are made regarding the project's
direction, codebase, and community.

## Decision-Making Authority

The Owner (see [`OWNER.md`](./OWNER.md)) holds final authority over:

- Product direction and roadmap prioritization
- Architecture and technical decisions
- Approval and merging of all code changes
- Access grants and revocations to this repository
- Licensing, trademark, and legal matters
- Acceptance or rejection of external contributions

## Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
| --- | --- |
| **Owner** | Final decision-making authority across product, engineering, legal, and brand. Currently Chris Odhiambo. |
| **Maintainers** | Individuals explicitly delegated review/merge authority by the Owner for specific areas of the codebase. None are delegated by default. |
| **Contributors** | Individuals or organizations with a written contributor agreement, permitted to submit changes per [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](./CONTRIBUTING.md). |
| **Community members** | Anyone reporting issues, asking questions, or participating in discussions, without write access to the codebase. |

## How Changes Get Made

1. Significant changes are proposed via an issue or discussion before
   implementation begins.
2. The Owner (or a delegated maintainer) reviews and either approves
   the direction or requests changes.
3. Implementation proceeds via pull request, following
   [`CONTRIBUTING.md`](./CONTRIBUTING.md).
4. The Owner or a delegated maintainer reviews and merges. No pull
   request is merged without this review.

## Delegation

The Owner may delegate maintainer status to trusted individuals for
specific areas of the project. Delegation is explicit, documented, and
revocable at the Owner's discretion. Absent explicit delegation, no
default maintainer authority exists beyond the Owner.

## Changes to This Document

This governance model may be revised by the Owner as the project and
team evolve. Material changes will be reflected here and noted in
[`CHANGELOG.md`](./CHANGELOG.md).
